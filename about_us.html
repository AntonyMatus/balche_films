About Us
Passionate creators dedicated to bringing compelling narratives to life.

Founded by Mexican filmmaker Luis Quijano, Balché is a film production company. Inspired by the Mayan drink and tree from which the drink comes from, Balché is focused on telling  profound and impactful narratives in genre filmmaking, mostly horror and fantasy, that showcase the complexities of human nature.

Balché runs away from bland and plastic styles, instead focusing on stories out of the ordinary, open to explore non-traditional dimensions, and exposing the good, the bad, the hopeful and the bleak of the world.

Our Founder

Luis Quijano is a Mexican filmmaker born in Mérida, Yucatán, currently based in Los Angeles. Since 2015, he has produced, written and directed narrative films, music videos and commercials both in Mexico and in the United States.

His directorial work commonly explores themes of fear, belief and social norms in culturally driven stories led by Latin-American characters within the horror genre and founding Balché Films is his way of continuing to tell these stories in a high profile manner.

“We know the thrills and joys that a film can give its audience and we respect the viewer’s time, money and effort. Whether it’s a film, a music video or a commercial, whether it’s a horror, a drama, a comedy, we hope to bring audiences around the world an unforgettable experience.”

Get to know Luis Quijano

In addition, he is an alumni of the Tomorrow's Filmmakers Today Fellowship presented by HBO & the Hollywood Foreign Press Association, a member of the 2023 class of Linterna Lab, a recipient of the NALIP emerging content creator scholarship in 2019, 2021 & 2022, and he has screened his work in over a dozen countries, including a screening at the Academy of Motion Pictures Arts & Sciences for the “In Focus: Hispanic & Latinx Cinema" event presented by New Filmmakers Los Angeles with his short film “Caminante, Caminante: La Leyenda del Huay Chivo"

Currently, Luis is in post production of his short film “Avem” and in development of his feature films “The Bugs Inside”, “This is Who We Are” and “Caminante, Caminante”, based on his short film of the same name.

Our Workflow process

Our Workflow Process
1. Development

While it may take months or even years for a project to leave this stage, from fleshing out ideas, to writing the first drafts, figuring out upcoming logistics of the production, to funding, we take great care of this stage of the production and wholeheartedly enjoy it. In our eyes, the screenwriter of the project is the leader of this stage.

Our Workflow Process
2. Pre-Production

With funding and a greenlight secured, the producer and director begin preparing for filming: cast and crew is hired, the budget is finalized, script is locked, equipment is secured, insurances are locked, locations are scouted, the shooting schedule is set, among other tasks. By the end of this stage, the producer and director are ready to shoot and have everything they need for a smooth, and enjoyable shoot.

Our Workflow Process
3. Production

The fun part. Where magic happens. The film is produced and shot. All the work put in development and pre production reflects itself at this stage. We give full creative control and trust to the director but we also believe in the power of collaboration and safe communication. No matter how low or high the budget is, or how big or small the crew is, the film gets made with the resources available always with the aim for it to connect with audiences around the world.

Our Workflow Process
4. Post-Production

The last stage of the creation of the film. Where magic continues. Once the film is shot (or while it’s shot), the footage is sent over to the film editor. In this process, the footage is assembled to create a finished, completed cut of the film. The production sound is edited, the music and songs are composed and recorded as well as the sound effects, foley, visual effects, among others. Once everything is edited together, and the director and producers approve the cut, the film is locked and ready for the sales, release and distribution phase. Every project has a unique post time and we stand by the fact that this stage can’t be rushed and that this is a key stage for the film to have a long life after release.

Frequently Asked Questions of our Leads

How can I get in touch for collaboration or project inquiries?
Write to us from our contact page.
Does Balché Films accept script submissions from external writers?
No, we won’t read any unsolicited scripts but we are open to meet or talk for future collaborations.
Does Balché Films only produce horror films?
While our focus is mostly genre filmmaking, we are open to producing all types of films as long as they go along with our storytelling goals and mission.
Does Balché Films provide film financing or co-production opportunities?
Yes, Balché Films is proud to say that we’ve financed and co-produced over a dozen productions. Our partners The Yucatan Consulting Group and Humpback Whale Studios are two companies we’ve co-produced projects with and we’ve shared great success together.
Where can we watch Balché’s films?
Most finished films are available on the Our Projects page. Some are not available on the website but, if interested to watch one that’s unavailable, a private link can be sent to be able to watch it.
